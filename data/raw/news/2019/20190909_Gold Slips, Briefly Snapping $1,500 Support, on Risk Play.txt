There was also fresh evidence from China that the flood of central bank buying that drove gold's rally in the first half of the year may be drying up. China’s central bank raised its gold holdings to 62.45 million ounces in August from 62.26 million ounces a month earlier, according to data on its website at the weekend. However, that represented purchases of only 5.91 tons in the month, after an average of 11.75 tons a month since December.
