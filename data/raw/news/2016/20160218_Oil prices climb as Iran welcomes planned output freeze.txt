Title: Oil prices climb as Iran welcomes planned output freeze
Date: 20160218
Content:
Investing.com - Oil prices rose on Thursday as Iran welcomed plans by Russia and Saudi Arabia to cap production to prop up the market. Global benchmark Brent rose 0.88% to $34.81 a barrel by 09.51 GMT. U.S. crude futures rose 1.94% to $31.26 a barrel. But analysts said the move was unlikely to lead to output cuts and Iran refused to limit its own output.
