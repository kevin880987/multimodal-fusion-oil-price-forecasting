Title: Oil falls on U.S. rig count jump
Date: 20170123
Content:
Investing.com - Oil fell Monday as a jump in the U.S. rig count outweighed an upbeat output cut compliance meeting.Brent crude shed 48 cents, or 0.87%, to $55.01 at 07:45 ET. U.S. crude lost 62 cents, or 1.16%, to $52.60.Baker Hughes U.S. rig count data Friday showed a jump of 29 to 551, the highest level in 14 months.That could point to higher U.S. shale output, which could offset the positive impact of the output cuts.OPEC and non-OPEC producers held a meeting on the implementation of the cuts over the weekend. The accord calls for a reduction of some 1.8 million barrels a day in the first half of this year.Saudi Energy Minister Khalid al-Falih estimated the level of compliance at already 80%.
