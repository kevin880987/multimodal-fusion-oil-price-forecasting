Title: NewsBreak: U.S. Crude Inventories Rose by 3.1M Barrels Last Week
Date: 20191002
Content:
Investing.com - U.S. oil inventories showed a gain of 3.1 million barrels last week, the Energy Information Administration said Wednesday, more than the market was anticipating, which pressured oil prices. Analysts were expecting a rise of about 1.57 million barrels of crude for the week ended Sept. 27, according to forecasts compiled by Investing.com. The EIA said gasoline inventories for the week fell by about 230,000 barrels, confounding forecasts for a build of about 450,000 barrels. Distillate stockpiles dropped by about 2.4 million barrels. Analysts had been looking for a decline of about 1.8 million barrels. WTI futures fell 1.5% to $52.84 just after the report. They were down about 0.2% at $53.51 before the numbers were released.
