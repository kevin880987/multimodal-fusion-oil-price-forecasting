Title: Saudi, Kuwaiti remarks raise hopes for output cut extension
Date: 20170420
Content:
Investing.com - Remarks by Saudi and Kuwaiti officials Thursday raised hopes for output cut deal extension."I expect we will have an extension," Kuwait’s oil minister Essam al-Marzouq said.OPEC and non-OPEC producers are cutting output by 1.8 mn bpd in the first half.Despite the cuts, inventories remain high as U.S. output increases.Marzouq noted increased compliance of non-OPEC producers with the cuts.This shows the “importance of extending the agreement,” he added.Saudi Oil Minister Khalid al-Falih said there was "an initial agreement" on the need to extend cuts."Our target is the level of inventories,” al-Falih said.“This is the main indicator for the success of the initiative," he added.OPEC, non-OPEC producers to discuss an extension of the deal on May 25. WTI was up 0.59% at $51.15 at 04:45 ET after sharp losses overnight.
