Title: Gold Prices Down Amid Strong Equity Markets
Date: 20190819
Content:
Investing.com - Gold prices fell on Monday in Asia as Asian equities traded higher. The benchmark gold futures contract on New York Mercantile Exchange’s Comex was down 0.3% to $1,519.65 by 1:20 AM ET (05:20 GMT). Wednesday’s release of the Fed meeting minutes for July, along with the Thursday-through-Saturday Jackson Hole retreat, and Chairman Jay Powell’s speech on Friday are expected to dictate gold price movement this week. Despite the losses today, analysts said safe-haven demand for gold remained strong. “Precious metals may have benefited from equity angst and recession fears this week, but the lack of bad news is spurring some consolidation,” TD Securities said in a note on gold. Since the start of August, gold has gained over 6%, or about $90, on heightened trade tensions and sustained buying by central banks responding to a slew of disappointing economic data globally.
