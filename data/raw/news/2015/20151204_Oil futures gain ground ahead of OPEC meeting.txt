Title: Oil futures gain ground ahead of OPEC meeting
Date: 20151204
Content:
Investing.com - U.S. oil futures gained ground on Friday, amid hopes the Organization of Petroleum Exporting Countries will announced production cuts at a meeting scheduled later in the day. U.S. crude futures for January delivery were last at $41.70 a barrel, up 1.50% for the day. On the ICE Futures Exchange in London, the January Brent contract were up 1.60% at $44.54 a barrel. Crude oil strengthened as media reports said that Saudi Arabia, which has so far resisted pressure to waver from its no-cut policy, will propose a supply cut of 1 million barrels per day, as long as other OPEC producers like Iran and Iraq, and non-OPEC members such as Russia and Mexico join them. But gains were limited as the U.S. dollar regained strength after Federal Reserve Chair Janet Yellen strongly indicated to Congress on Thursday that Fed policymakers are likely to vote to raise interest rates at its meeting in two weeks, barring any major shocks to the global economy. Dollar-denominated oil futures contracts tend to fall when the dollar rises, as this makes oil more expensive for buyers in other currencies.
