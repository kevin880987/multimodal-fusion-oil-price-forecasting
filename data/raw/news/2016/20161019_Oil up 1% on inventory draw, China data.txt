Title: Oil up 1% on inventory draw, China data
Date: 20161019
Content:
Oil was up 1% Wednesday after weekly industry figures showed a surprise fall in U.S. crude stocks.U.S. crude was up 69 cents, or 1,37%, at $50.98 at 07:00 ET, while Brent crude gained 1.35% to $52.38.American Petroleum Institute (API) figures Tuesday showed a drop in U.S. crude inventories of 3.8 million barrels to 467.1 million.Energy Information Administration (EIA) figures are due out later Wednesday.Chinese oil output fell in September, while third-quarter GDP grew 6.7%, lending further support.OPEC secretary general Mohammed Barkindo said he was optimistic of the cartel agreeing to a planned output cut next month.The dollar was index was lower. A weaker dollar supports demand for oil
