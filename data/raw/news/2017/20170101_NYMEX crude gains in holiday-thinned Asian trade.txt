Title: NYMEX crude gains in holiday-thinned Asian trade
Date: 20170101
Content:
Investing.com - U.S. crude oil prices drifted higher in Asia on Monday with most financial markets in the region closed to mark the New Year holiday. Crude for February delivery on the New York Mercantile Exchange rose 0.24% to $53.90 a barrel. Global benchmark Brent crude traded on London's Intercontinental Exchange was last quoted at $56.74 a barrel. Investors await news of the adherence to combined production cuts by OPEC and non-OPEC nations near 1.8 million barrels per day that started at the New Year. A meeting in Kuwait later this month will assess progress.
