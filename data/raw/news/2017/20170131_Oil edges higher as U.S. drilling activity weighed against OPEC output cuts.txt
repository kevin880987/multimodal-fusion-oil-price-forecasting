Title: Oil edges higher as U.S. drilling activity weighed against OPEC output cuts
Date: 20170131
Content:
Investing.com - Oil edged higher Tuesday as investors weighed the impact of higher U.S. drilling activity against output cuts.Brent crude was up 21 cents, or 0.38%, at $55.53 at 08:00 ET. U.S. crude added 4 cents, or 0.08%, to $52.67.A pick-up in North American shale output could offset the impact of agreed output cuts by other producers.OPEC and non-OPEC producers have agreed to cut output by some 1.8 million barrels a day in the first half of this year.A Reuters survey showed an 82% compliance level with OPEC's output cut quota in January.
