Title: Oil lower in cautious trade ahead of Brexit vote
Date: 20160621
Content:
Investing.com - Oil futures were lower early Tuesday after a strong two-day rally as caution set in ahead of the Brexit vote on Thursday.Brent crude held above the $50 mark but was trading down 0.93% at $50.18 at 06:45 ET. U.S. crude for August delivery fell 0.82% to $49.55. Polls released late Monday showed the outcome of the Brexit referendum in balance between “Remain” and “Leave.”The dollar index was slightly lower. A weaker dollar lends support to the oil market.Investors will be looking to semi-annual congressional testimony by Fed chief Janet Yellen later Tuesday. Saudi oil exports fell in April to 7.4 million barrels a day despite higher output.This suggests that supply continues to outstrip demand.The American Petroleum Institute is due to release the latest weekly U.S. crude inventory figures on Tuesday.
