Title: Oil market re-balancing seen accelerating: IEA
Date: 20170516
Content:
Investing.com - The IEA Tuesday said the oil market was almost balanced in the first quarter."Re-balancing is essentially here, and, in the short-term, is accelerating, the IEA said in its monthly report.The agency said if OPEC output is maintained at 31.8 mb/d in Q2 this would imply a stock draw of 700,000 barrels.But the International Energy Agency said inventories might not have returned to five-year average at the end of the year.Saudi Arabia and Russia Monday backed a 9-month extension of an output cut deal to March next year.A decision on the possible extension is expected at a meeting on May 25.OPEC and non-OPEC producers have agreed to cut output by 1.8 mb/d in the first half.The IEA noted increased output by the U.S. and possible higher production by Libya and Nigeria:The Paris-based agency left its demand growth forecast for this year unchanged at 1.3 mb/d. Brent crude was up 0.25% at $51.95 at 06:00 ET.
