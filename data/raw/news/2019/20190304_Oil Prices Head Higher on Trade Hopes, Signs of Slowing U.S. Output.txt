Title: Oil Prices Head Higher on Trade Hopes, Signs of Slowing U.S. Output
Date: 20190304
Content:
Investing.com - Crude prices traded higher on Monday amid news that the U.S. and China could be close to a trade deal and signs that the surge in American oil production might be slowing. New York-traded West Texas Intermediate crude futures rose 47 cents, or 0.84%, at $56.27 a barrel by 7:43 AM ET (12:43 GMT). Meanwhile, Brent crude futures, the benchmark for oil prices outside the U.S., traded up 55 cents, or 0.85%, to $67.39, backing off of $65.62 which was also its highest level in three months. U.S. President Donald Trump and Chinese President Xi Jinping could seal a formal trade deal at a summit around March 27, given progress in talks between the two countries, the Wall Street Journal reported on Sunday. The two countries appear close to a deal that would roll back U.S. tariffs on at least $200 billion worth of Chinese goods, while Beijing makes pledges on structural economic changes and eliminates retaliatory tariffs on U.S. goods. Traders are concerned that the prolonged dispute would have a negative impact the world’s two largest economies - particularly on China, the largest oil importer - depressing demand for the commodity. The market has also softened since the most recent weekly data from Baker Hughes showed that U.S. energy firms last week cut the number of drilling rigs to the lowest in almost nine months, suggesting early signs of moderating domestic production growth.  Barclays  (LON:BARC) analysts still warned over the weekend that U.S. oil output would likely increase in the second half of this year. In other energy trading, gasoline futures gained 1.03% to $1.7481 a gallon by 7:45 AM ET (12:45 GMT), while heating oil advanced 0.69% to $2.0149 a gallon. Lastly, natural gas futures inched up 0.03% to $2.860 per million British thermal unit. -- Reuters contributed to this report.
