Title: Oil higher on output cut extension hopes
Date: 20170428
Content:
Investing.com - Oil was higher Friday on renewed hopes of an extension of output cuts by major producers.U.S. crude was up 51 cents, or 1.04%, at $49.48 at 08:00 ET. Brent crude gained 52 cents, or 1.00%, to $52.34.Output cuts of 1.8 million barrels a day agreed by OPEC and non-OPEC producers have so far failed to make inroads into an inventories glut.OPEC and non-OPEC producers are expected to decide next month whether to extend the accord beyond June.Saudi Energy Minister Khalid al-Falih said Friday it was important to agree an extension.Higher crude output is offsetting the impact of the output cuts.Baker Hughes rig count data are due out later Friday.
