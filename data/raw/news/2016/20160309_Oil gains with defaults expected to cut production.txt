Title: Oil gains with defaults expected to cut production
Date: 20160309
Content:
Investing.com - Brent crude prices rose above $40 per barrel on Wednesday as traders expected defaults to trigger a drop in production. The growing number of defaults by energy companies since the start of the year is expected to lead to a dip in global production. But concerns over slowing demand and an ongoing global production and storage overhang was capping any potential for bigger price gains. The U.S. was to release data on oil stockpiles later on Wednesday.
