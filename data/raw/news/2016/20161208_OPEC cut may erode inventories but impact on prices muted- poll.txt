Title: OPEC cut may erode inventories but impact on prices muted: poll
Date: 20161208
Content:
Investing.com - OPEC's output cut may erode global stocks but not seen pushing prices much higher: Reuters pollOPEC agreed to cut output last month by 1.2 mn b/d to 32.5 mn from the start of next year.If deal fully implemented and prices rise this could spark higher output by non-OPEC producers.OPEC and non-OPEC producers set to meet this weekend on proposed output cut by the latter.Analysts polled see Brent crude average of $44.69 in 2016, $57.01 in 2017.The compares with $44.78 and $57.08 for the same periods prior to OPEC deal.Analysts see need for OPEC to prolong six-month accord to achieve rebalancing in H2 2017.
